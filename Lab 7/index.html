The Moon is Earth's only permanent natural satellite, it is the fifth-largest natural satellite in the Solar System, and the largest among planetary satellites relative to the size of the planet that it orbits. The Moon always shows Earth the same face, it is in synchronous rotation with Earth. Its near side is marked by large dark plains that fill the spaces between the bright ancient crustal highlands and the prominent impact craters. The Moon's surface is actually dark, although compared to the night sky, it appears very bright with a reflectance just slightly higher than that of worn asphalt. The Moon is moving approximately 3.8 cm away from our planet every year. It's gravitational influence produces the ocean tides, body tides, and the slight lengthening of the day.
